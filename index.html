Water Class 7 Geography Chapter 5
Water Cycle
Describes the natural cycle of water changing from liquid to vapor through evaporation, condensation, and precipitation.
Continual Transformation
Highlights the enduring presence of water as it cycles between oceans, atmosphere, and land over centuries.
Sources of Freshwater
Major sources of freshwater include rivers, ponds, springs, and glaciers, while oceans primarily contain salty water.
Salty Oceans
Oceans contain salty water due to a high concentration of dissolved salts, mainly sodium chloride (table salt).
Global Water Circulation
Emphasizes how water used in one location today can travel through various water bodies in the past, showcasing Earth's interconnected water systems.
Ocean Dynamics
Describes the continuous movement of ocean water in contrast to still ponds and lakes.
Types of Movements
Identifies ocean movements such as waves, tides, and currents, illustrating the dynamic nature of ocean circulation.
Seashore Experience
Portrays the sensory delights of the ocean environment, like wet sand, seabirds, and the rhythmic sound of waves.
Wave Characteristics
Describes waves as the alternating rise and fall of ocean water, often seen at the beach.
Beach Interaction
Highlights how waves playfully interact at the beach, washing objects like a ball back to the shore.
Storms Create Huge Waves
Winds during storms generate massive waves with destructive potential.
Tsunamis from Geological Events
Geological events like earthquakes trigger tsunamis, immensely destructive tidal waves.
Historic Tsunami
Describes the 2004 tsunami, a tragic event showcasing the power of tsunamis.
Tsunami Height
Mentions tsunamis can reach heights up to 150m, indicating their potential for devastation.
Tidal Mechanism
Tides are rhythmic ocean movements influenced by the sun and moon's gravitational forces.
Tide Types
High tides flood shores, low tides expose more beach area.
Tide Variation
Spring tides (full or new moons) create the highest tides, neap tides (moon's quarters) produce lower tides.
Practical Benefits
High tides aid navigation and fishing, while tidal energy is harnessed for electricity.
Definition of Ocean Currents
Ocean currents are continuous streams of water following specific paths on the ocean's surface.
Types of Currents
Warm currents originate near the equator, while cold currents come from polar regions.
Influence on Temperature
Warm ocean currents affect nearby land temperatures and climate.
Fishing and Climate Impact
Convergence of warm and cold currents creates prime fishing areas but can lead to foggy weather affecting navigation.

For Chapter 5 of Politics/ Civics Women's Change the World

Perceived Occupational Gender Roles
1. Gender stereotypes limit career choices, associating women with nurturing roles and men with technical jobs like the army.
2. These stereotypes can hinder girls' opportunities in fields such as medicine and engineering.
3. Social pressures often prioritize girls' marriage over education and professional aspirations.
Pressures on Children in Society
1. Children face societal pressures from adults and teasing from peers.
2. Boys often feel pressured to pursue high-paying jobs and conform to traditional male behavior.
3. Early conditioning discourages boys from openly expressing emotions like crying.
Breaking Stereotypes: Laxmi Lakra's Inspiring Journey
1. Laxmi Lakra, from Jharkhand, became the first female engine driver for Northern Railways.
2. Laxmi's parents prioritized her education, and she earned a diploma in electronics.
3. Her determination and love for challenges led her into traditionally male-dominated fields.
Learning for Change: Transforming Education Norms
1. Historically, education was limited, especially for girls.
2. The nineteenth century saw a shift with new ideas about education and schools emerging.
3. Women and men championed efforts to establish schools for girls and attain literacy and education.
The Remarkable Journey of Rashsundari Devi
1. Rashsundari Devi, a housewife from West Bengal, defied norms to read and write.
2. She secretly taught herself to read and write and authored "Amar Jiban," the first Indian woman's autobiography.
Challenges in Gender Disparities in Education
1. While boys and girls attend school, disparities in the quality of education and access persist.
2. Census data shows significant gender gaps in literacy rates.
3. Gender gaps continue to be a concern, particularly for Muslim girls and marginalized communities.
4. Discrimination against Dalit, Adivasi, and Muslim children contributes to disparities in educational opportunities.
Tribes, Nomads, and Settled Communities Class -7 History chapter 5
Caste-based Societies
Society in large parts of the subcontinent was structured by the rules of varna, prescribed by the Brahmanas and accepted by rulers of kingdoms, resulting in increasing social and economic disparities.
Under the Delhi Sultans and Mughals, these hierarchical social classes became more pronounced.
Tribal Societies
Other societies, referred to as tribes, did not conform to Brahmanical rules and were not divided into distinct classes.
Tribes were united by kinship and engaged in diverse activities like agriculture, hunting, gathering, and herding, often residing in remote areas.
These tribes collectively controlled land and resources, with rules unique to their culture.
The subcontinent was home to various influential tribes living in forests, hills, deserts, and remote regions, sometimes clashing with caste-based societies.
Interactions and Dependence
Caste-based and tribal societies had a complex relationship of conflict and dependence, influencing mutual change.
Recording Tribal Histories
Tribal people did not maintain written records but passed down rich customs and oral traditions, now used by present-day historians to document tribal histories.
Diversity of Tribal People
Tribes were present in nearly every region, with varying influence.
Influential tribes included the Khokhars, Gakkhars, Langahs, Arghuns, and others in different regions.
Eastern regions had Chero chiefdoms, with tribes like Mundas and Santals. The western and southern regions had tribes like Kolis, Berads, Koragas, Vetars, and Bhils, who were engaged in varied activities.
Some Bhils became settled agriculturists and zamindars, while Gonds were numerous in Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Andhra Pradesh.
